Each number is a coordinate. Together they define a direction from the origin.
step 2 — magnitudes (lengths)
|A| = √(9 + 4) = 3.61
|B| = √(1 + 16) = 4.12
Pythagoras — the "ruler length" of each arrow.
step 3 — dot product
A·B = (3×1) + (2×4)
     = 3 + 8 = 11
Multiply matching components, sum them. Bakes in both angle and magnitude.
step 4 — cosine similarity
cos θ = A·B / (|A| × |B|)
      = 11 / (3.61 × 4.12)
      = 11 / 14.87
      = 0.74
Dividing by the magnitudes strips out length — leaving only direction.
step 5 — the angle
θ = arccos(0.74) = 42°
